Gondor

The most prominent kingdom of Men in Middle-earth, Gondor is known for its impressive capital city of Minas Tirith, which is built on seven levels and overlooks the Pelennor Fields.

The Shire is home to hobbits, a small and peaceful race of beings who live in underground homes called hobbit-holes. It is known for its rolling hills, lush farmland and cozy villages.

Erebor

Erebor, also known as the Lonely Mountain, is a great mountain located to the east of the Misty Mountains. Erebor is famous for its vast wealth of gold and precious gems, which were mined by the dwarves.

Rivendell

Rivendell, also known as Imladris, is an Elven stronghold located in a valley in the foothills of the Misty Mountains. The valley is hidden from view and protected by powerful magic, making it difficult to find for those who are not invited.

Mordor

Mordor is a barren and desolate land located in the southeast of Middle-earth. It was created by the dark lord Sauron as his stronghold and base of operations during the War of the Ring and is know for its harsh climate, volcanic activity and toxic air.
